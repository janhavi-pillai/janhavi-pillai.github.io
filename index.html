Janhavi Pillai

I'm a Staff Research Associate at the UCSF ALBA Lab and the UCSF Dyslexia Center which is led by Dr. Maria Luisa Gorno Tempini. I work with multimodal neuroimaging data (structural MRI, functional MRI, diffusion MRI tractography) to better understand the neural underpinnings of how language and memory are affected in two syndromes: Primary Progressive Aphasia and Dyslexia. I am interested in exploring the intersection of machine learning and health. How can we build algorithmic tools to enhance patient diagnosis and care?

Originally from the Bay Area, I graduated from UC Berkeley in 2022 with a bachelors degree in Cognitive Science and a minor in Data Science. I researched the role of certain brain areas (PVN and vHPC) in fear and anxiety related behaviors in mice using calcium imaging data at the UCSF Kheirbek Lab where I was supervised by Victoria Turner. I also worked part time at the CITRIS Foundry, a startup incubator at UC Berkeley helping UC scientists, engineers, researchers to form and accelerate venture-ready startups.

Activation of Ventral Hippocampus and Paraventricular Nucleus of the Hypothalamus during Anxiety-Related Behaviors.
Janhavi Pillai, Victoria Turner, Dr. Mazen Kheirbek
UC Berkeley Cognitive Science Undergraduate Honors Thesis, advised by Victoria Turner, Dr. David Presti and Dr. Mazen Kheirbek
Thesis

We aimed to investigate and compare the roles of the PVN and vHPC during specific fear and anxiety related behaviors by examining mice PVN and vHPC activation occurring during four specific behaviors (head dip, elongation with locomotion, elongation with retraction, and turning) observed in the elevated plus maze (EPM) using calcium imaging. We found that the vHPC and PVN activations aligned in the direction of change for the elongation with retraction, head dip, and turning behaviors but differed for the elongation with locomotion behavior. The data was collected at the University of California, San Francisco.
